# 🎉 Things to do in Boise (Feb 25–27, 2026)

Whether you're arriving early or staying a few days after the conference, Boise offers plenty of winter-friendly activities, food, drinks, and local culture.

---

## 🎭 Arts, Culture & History

### 🖼️ [Boise Art Museum](https://www.boiseartmuseum.org/)
Contemporary art and sculpture garden in Julia Davis Park.  
📍 670 Julia Davis Dr | ⏰ 10 AM – 5 PM | 💲$6 admission

### 🏛️ [Idaho State Museum](https://history.idaho.gov/museum/)
Interactive exhibits on Idaho’s environment, Indigenous heritage, and state history.  
📍 610 Julia Davis Dr | ⏰ 10 AM – 5 PM | 💲$10 admission

### 🇪🇸 [The Basque Block](https://www.boisebasqueblock.com/)
Explore Boise’s Basque heritage with food, wine, museums, and music.  
Don’t miss [Bar Gernika](https://www.bargernika.com/) for croquetas and cider!

## 🍽️ Great Places to Eat

### 🥗 [Fork](https://www.boisefork.com/)
Farm-to-table American comfort food in a modern space.  
📍 199 N 8th St | Reservations recommended

### 🍕 [The Wylder](https://thewylderboise.com/)
Neapolitan-style pizza, small plates, and cocktails.  
📍 501 W Broad St

### 🥙 [Bar Gernika](https://www.bargernika.com/)
Boise’s iconic Basque pub — cozy and casual with amazing lamb grinders.  
📍 202 S Capitol Blvd

---

## 🍺 Breweries & Cideries

### 🍻 [Payette Brewing Company](https://www.payettebrewing.com/)
Spacious indoor/outdoor taproom with food trucks and seasonal releases.  
📍 733 S Pioneer St

### 🍻 [Boise Brewing](https://www.boisebrewing.com/)
Community-focused downtown brewery with award-winning stouts and IPAs.  
📍 521 W Broad St

### 🍺 [White Dog Brewing](https://whitedogbrewing.com/boise/)
Modern taproom downtown with a wide beer selection and games.  
📍 705 W Fulton St

### 🍏 [Meriwether Cider Co.](https://www.meriwethercider.com/)
Family-run cidery with adventurous flavors like ginger, huckleberry, and hopped ciders.  
📍 224 N 9th St

---

## 🍷 Wineries & Tasting Rooms

### 🍷 [Telaya Wine Co.](https://www.telayawine.com/)
Beautiful riverfront tasting room just outside downtown.  
📍 240 E 32nd St, Garden City

### 🍷 [Cinder Wines](https://www.cinderwines.com/)
Award-winning urban winery specializing in Rhône-style wines.  
📍 107 E 44th St, Garden City

### 🍷 [Coiled Wines](https://www.coiledwines.com/)
Trendy boutique winery known for Rieslings and bubbly.  
📍 3408 W Chinden Blvd

> 💡 *Garden City’s “Surel Mitchell Live-Work-Create District” is a cool area to explore for wine, beer, art, and riverfront walks.*

---

## 🥾 Outdoor Options (Weather Permitting)

### 🌿 [Boise River Greenbelt](https://www.cityofboise.org/departments/parks-and-recreation/parks/boise-greenbelt/)
Paved walking and biking trail that winds through downtown along the river.

### 🏞️ [Camel’s Back Park & Hyde Park](https://www.cityofboise.org/parks-and-rec/parks/camels-back-park/)
Views of the foothills plus a charming neighborhood with coffee, shopping, and dining.

### 🎿 [Bogus Basin Mountain Recreation Area](https://bogusbasin.org/)
Just 45 minutes from Boise — skiing, snowshoeing, and tubing.  
📍 2600 N Bogus Basin Rd

---

## 🚕 Getting Around

- Downtown Boise is **walkable** and compact.
- [Valley Regional Transit](https://www.valleyregionaltransit.org/) offers bus service.
- Rideshare apps (Uber, Lyft) are widely available.
- E-scooters (Lime, Bird) operate downtown, weather permitting.
